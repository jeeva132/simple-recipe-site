Dosa

Dosa is a popular South Indian crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter. It's a delicious and versatile dish that can be enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. Learn how to make crispy and flavorful dosas at home with this easy recipe.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

20 min.

serving

4 servings

Tags : dosa south indian breakfast vegetarian

instructions

step 1

Wash and soak rice and urad dal (black gram lentils) separately in water for at least 4-5 hours or overnight.

step 2

Drain the water and grind the soaked rice and urad dal separately to a smooth batter. Combine both batters in a mixing bowl, add salt, and mix well. Let the batter ferment for 8-10 hours or until it's doubled in volume.

step 3

Heat a non-stick skillet or dosa pan over medium-high heat. Grease it lightly with oil. Pour a ladleful of the dosa batter in the center and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin, even dosa. Cook until the edges turn golden brown and crispy.

step 4

Flip the dosa and cook the other side for a minute or until it's cooked through. Repeat the process for the remaining batter.

step 5

Serve hot dosas with coconut chutney and sambar.

ingredients

1 cup rice

1/2 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)

Salt to taste

Oil for greasing

tools

Grinder

Mixing Bowl

Non-stick Skillet or Dosa Pan

Ladle
